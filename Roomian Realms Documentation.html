Cosmic Origins & Lore

The Wormlings are not native to the world of Syx, but rather entities from beyond the dimensional veil. They arrived through an event known as "The Breach," a catastrophic tear in reality that allowed these parasitic beings to enter this dimension.

Ruled by the mysterious God-Emperor of the Endless Void, the Wormlings seek to recreate The Breach in reverse, allowing them to return to their home dimension with the knowledge and resources they've gathered from Syx.

Their worship of Shmalor is pragmatic rather than devout‚Äîthey recognize in her death a parallel to their own separation from their home dimension, and they believe her power can help them achieve their ultimate goal.

Society & Culture

Wormling society is hierarchical and based on a hive-mind structure. All Wormlings share a connection to the collective consciousness, though individuals maintain autonomy for practical purposes.

They organize into "nests" led by Elder Hosts‚ÄîWormlings who have successfully inhabited powerful beings for extended periods. These Elders direct infiltration operations and oversee the establishment of void-gates, small-scale breaches that allow limited communication with their home dimension.

Reproduction occurs through parasitism, with Wormling larvae implanted into host bodies. The process eventually consumes the host, producing multiple new Wormlings. Some hosts, however, are preserved and controlled rather than consumed, serving as valuable infiltrators.

Gameplay Tips

When playing as Wormlings:

  • Focus on espionage and infiltration rather than direct confrontation
  • Establish underground chambers for breeding and void-gate construction
  • Maintain a facade of normalcy in your settlement to avoid detection
  • Use captured hosts strategically for diplomatic missions
  • Research void technologies to strengthen your connection to your home dimension
  • Balance between expansion and secrecy‚Äîgrowing too quickly will alert others to your presence

Origins & Lore

The Bloodelves were once forest-dwelling hunters who were transformed by Aminion's dark essence. This transformation granted them immortality and powerful abilities, but at the cost of requiring blood consumption to survive.

They suffer from a condition known as "the Withering" if they do not regularly consume blood, causing their bodies to deteriorate rapidly. This necessity has shaped their entire society around the acquisition and management of blood sources.

While primarily devoted to Aminion, some Bloodelves maintain connections to Shmalor worship, seeing parallels between their own transformation and the slain goddess's potential resurrection.

Society & Culture

Bloodelf society is strictly hierarchical, with status determined by blood magic potency and the quality of one's blood slaves. The most powerful Bloodelves can go longer without feeding and possess greater magical abilities.

Their settlements are built around "Heartwood Trees" - ancient trees fed with blood sacrifices that produce crimson sap enhancing Bloodelf abilities. These trees serve as both religious and practical centers of their communities.

Art and architecture among Bloodelves emphasize their predatory nature, with motifs of veins, arteries, and flowing blood common in their designs. Their music often incorporates the rhythms of heartbeats, creating hypnotic effects that aid in hunting and subduing prey.

Gameplay Tips

When playing as Bloodelves:

  • Prioritize establishing blood farms for sustainable feeding
  • Develop archery and stealth units for superior ranged combat
  • Plant and nurture Heartwood Trees in strategic locations
  • Conduct night raids to capture slaves rather than killing enemies
  • Research blood magic technologies for unique advantages
  • Form alliances with Aminion-worshipping factions while being wary of Athuri followers

Origins & Lore

The Satyrs were among the first races created by the Astari, designed to be guardians of the natural world. Their society evolved to be led by females, with the wisest and most skilled hunters becoming the revered Matrons who guide their communities.

While outwardly peaceful and defensive, the Satyrs harbor a darker nature that emerges during the Wild Hunt ritual. Under the full moon, selected warriors transform into predatory hunters, becoming ferocious and single-minded in their pursuit. No outsider who has witnessed a Wild Hunt has ever lived to tell of it.

Some scholars suspect that the Wild Hunt may be connected to an older, darker faith that predates their worship of Athuri, though the Satyrs themselves never speak of such things to outsiders.

Society & Culture

Satyr society is matriarchal, with communities led by councils of Matrons who make decisions through consensus. Males typically serve as craftsmen, gatherers, and caregivers, while females form the backbone of defense and hunting parties.

Their settlements are built in harmony with the forest, often incorporating living trees and natural formations. They practice sustainable forestry and hunting, taking only what they need and ensuring the continued health of their environment.

The most feared warriors among the Satyrs are the Moonrage Matrons, elite female fighters who have mastered the art of controlled transformation during the Wild Hunt. They wield the legendary Moonbane arrows, which are said to never miss their mark when fired under moonlight.

Gameplay Tips

When playing as Satyrs:

  • Focus on defensive structures rather than offensive capabilities

Origins & History

The Hellspawns were created when Aminion, the fallen Astari, corrupted a portion of the world with his chaotic essence. From this corruption emerged the Tilapi, beings of fire and malice who serve as the physical manifestation of Aminion's desire to unmake creation.

Their creation myth tells that they were born from the blood of Aminion himself, spilled during his battle with Athuri. Each drop that touched the ground birthed a Hellspawn, making them consider themselves the true children of their dark god.

The most infamous of their kind was Trinduri, a Tilapi chieftain who slew the goddess Shmalor during the first war of the gods. This act cemented the eternal enmity between Hellspawns and all followers of Shmalor.

Society & Culture

Hellspawn society is built on a strict hierarchy of power and cruelty. Leadership is determined through combat, with the strongest and most ruthless rising to positions of authority. Challenges to leadership are common, making their power structure volatile and ever-changing.

Their settlements are centered around temples to Aminion, where blood sacrifices are regularly performed to maintain their connection to their creator. These temples feature massive fire pits that burn eternally, fed by both fuel and sacrificial offerings.

Art among the Hellspawns focuses on depictions of battle, conquest, and suffering. Their architecture emphasizes intimidation, with jagged spires, skull motifs, and structures that seem to defy natural laws. Music consists primarily of war drums and guttural chanting used to work themselves into a battle frenzy.

Gameplay Tips

When playing as Hellspawns:

Art among Froggians focuses on the beauty of natural patterns and mathematical precision. Their music utilizes the unique acoustics of water and air, creating harmonies impossible for land-dwelling races to replicate. They are also known for their intricate clockwork mechanisms and water-powered devices that serve both practical and artistic purposes.

Their settlements are centered around training grounds, meditation gardens, and simple but elegant architecture that emphasizes harmony with nature. Buildings are constructed to facilitate training, with many structures incorporating bars for pull-ups and open spaces for practice.

Daily life revolves around a strict schedule of training, meditation, and communal work. All Pasunkas, regardless of their role in society, are expected to maintain physical discipline through daily exercise routines. Their diet consists primarily of protein-rich foods and vegetables, with strict prohibitions against intoxicants that might cloud the mind or weaken the body.
