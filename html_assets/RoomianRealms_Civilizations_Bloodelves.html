Ancient forest predators transformed by Aminion's dark essence, the Bloodelves were once a noble elven race before being corrupted during the first war of the gods. They now sustain themselves through blood consumption, requiring regular feeding to prevent a degenerative condition known as "the Withering."

Their hunting prowess is unmatched, with the ability to track prey for days without rest. Their society is hierarchical, with status determined by blood magic potency and lineage purity. The oldest and most powerful Bloodelves, known as "Crimson Lords," rule from shadow-cloaked citadels deep within ancient forests.

Their settlements are built around "Heartwood Trees" fed with blood sacrifices, producing crimson sap that enhances their magical abilities. These trees are sacred to them, believed to connect directly to Aminion's realm. They practice a form of immortality through blood consumption, with the oldest among them dating back to the first war of the gods.

While they primarily worship Aminion, they maintain a secondary devotion to Shmalor, believing that the two deities share a covenant of blood and shadow. This dual worship is unique among the races of Syx and contributes to their complex religious practices.

Cultural Notes +

  • Bloodelves maintain elaborate hunting rituals, with specific ceremonies for different prey
  • They practice "blood art," using blood as pigment for elaborate tapestries and murals
  • Their architecture favors tall, slender structures with minimal windows to limit sunlight
  • They communicate through a combination of spoken language and subtle blood scent markers
  • They maintain extensive genealogical records, tracing bloodlines back thousands of years
  • They practice a form of blood communion where memories can be shared through ritual consumption

Historical Events +

  • The Crimson Transformation: When Aminion's essence first corrupted their race
  • The Blood Covenant: Their pact with Shmalor establishing dual worship
  • The Great Thirst: A period of extreme blood shortage that nearly destroyed their race
  • The Shadow War: A centuries-long conflict with the Satyrs over forest territories
  • The Heartwood Ritual: Discovery of the process to create and maintain Heartwood Trees

Gameplay Approach

Bloodelves excel at hunting, archery, and blood magic. Their extremely long lifespan means they accumulate skills and experience, making them formidable specialists. They are best suited for settlements with access to hunting grounds and with a steady supply of sacrifices for their blood rituals.

Settlement Strategy +

  • Early Game: Establish hunting lodges and secure a steady food supply
  • Mid Game: Develop blood altars and archery ranges
  • Late Game: Focus on blood magic research and elite military units
  • Population Management: Slow growth but extremely long-lived
  • Economy: Hunting, slave labor, and luxury goods should be primary income sources

Military Strategy +

  • Army Composition: Elite archers supported by blood mages and ambush specialists
  • Tactics: Ambush, hit-and-run, and night operations
  • Strengths: Superior ranged combat, stealth, and magical abilities
  • Weaknesses: Vulnerable to holy magic and blunt weapons
  • Special: Blood mages can drain enemy vitality in combat

Unique Buildings +

  • Blood Altar: Central facility for blood magic rituals and research
  • Heartwood Grove: Sacred site that enhances blood magic and extends lifespan
  • Shadow Archery Range: Produces elite archers with enhanced abilities
  • Crimson Library: Repository of blood magic knowledge and ancient texts
