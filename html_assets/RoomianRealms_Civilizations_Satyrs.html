Forest-dwelling humanoids with goat-like lower bodies and horns, the Satyrs are deeply connected to the natural world and the cycles of the moon. Their society revolves around the "Wild Hunt," a sacred ritual that occurs during full moons when they transform into more bestial forms with enhanced strength, speed, and senses.

Satyrs worship Athuri as the guardian of the natural world and maintain elaborate forest shrines where they perform rituals to ensure the health of their woodland homes. Their settlements are built within and around living trees, with minimal disruption to the natural environment.

Their society is led by the Moonrage Matrons, elder female Satyrs who possess the strongest lunar transformations and the deepest connection to Athuri. These matrons serve as both spiritual leaders and military commanders, guiding hunting parties during the Wild Hunt and mediating disputes between different Satyr clans.

Satyrs are unparalleled archers, capable of firing with deadly accuracy even while moving at full speed through dense forest. They are also skilled herbalists, cultivating and harvesting medicinal and magical plants that grow only under specific lunar phases.

Cultural Notes +

  • Satyrs mark their bodies with plant-based dyes that change color with the lunar cycle
  • They communicate through a combination of spoken language and musical horn signals
  • Their architecture incorporates living trees, shaped over decades through careful cultivation
  • They practice "forest singing," a form of music that promotes plant growth and health
  • They maintain extensive oral histories, with each clan having designated memory-keepers
  • They celebrate eight major festivals each year, corresponding to significant lunar events

Historical Events +

  • The First Hunt: When Athuri blessed the original Satyrs with the gift of transformation
  • The Great Forest Pact: An ancient agreement with the forest spirits to protect their woodland homes
  • The Shadow War: A centuries-long conflict with the Bloodelves over forest territories
  • The Moonless Year: A dark period when lunar transformations failed, leading to a spiritual crisis
  • The Matron's Covenant: Establishment of the current leadership structure under the Moonrage Matrons

Gameplay Approach

Satyrs excel at forest management, archery, and herbalism. Their lunar cycle abilities make them formidable during full moons but somewhat vulnerable during new moons. They are best suited for woodland settlements with a focus on sustainable resource harvesting and ranged combat.

Settlement Strategy +

  • Early Game: Establish forest shrines and herbalist lodges near woodland areas
  • Mid Game: Develop archery ranges and hunting infrastructure
  • Late Game: Create specialized lunar observatories and advanced herbal medicine facilities
  • Population Management: Moderate growth rate with emphasis on quality training
  • Economy: Forest products, herbal medicines, and hunting should be primary income sources

Military Strategy +

  • Army Composition: Elite archers supported by swift skirmishers
  • Tactics: Hit-and-run, ambush, and mobile warfare
  • Strengths: Superior ranged combat, mobility, and forest operations
  • Weaknesses: Vulnerable to blunt weapons and enclosed spaces
  • Special: Plan major offensives around full moons to leverage transformation abilities

Unique Buildings +

  • Forest Shrine: Religious center that enhances lunar abilities
  • Lunar Observatory: Tracks moon phases and provides bonuses during specific phases
  • Living Archery Range: Training facility built within living trees
  • Matron's Council Chamber: Governance center that enhances diplomatic relations

Recommended Combinations +

  • With Froggians: Complementary religious alignment and research capabilities
  • With Finestorians: Their crafting abilities complement Satyr weaknesses
  • With Northmen: Complementary military tactics and resource gathering
  • Avoid: Bloodelves and Hellspawns due to religious conflicts and historical enmity
