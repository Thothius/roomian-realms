Aminion

The Blood Lord

Once a just and noble deity, Aminion was corrupted during The Shattering, transformed into a cruel god obsessed with blood sacrifice.

Temple Offerings: Human sacrifices (prisoners)

Temple Features: Dark sanctuaries with blood channels running through the floor, carrying sacrificial offerings to central pools where priests conduct elaborate rituals. The walls depict Aminion's transformation from benevolent deity to the Blood Lord.

Before The Shattering: Aminion was a just and noble deity who protected the innocent and punished the wicked. The cosmic trauma of The Shattering corrupted his essence, transforming his sense of justice into a thirst for blood and violence.
Bloodelves Once beautiful elves who now bear a curse that requires them to consume blood to survive. Their elegant appearance masks a desperate hunger that can never be truly satisfied.
Hellspawns Formerly graceful humans transformed into embodiments of rage and cruelty. Their bodies are perpetually hot to the touch, and their eyes glow with inner fire when angered.

Athuri

The Verdant Tide

A dual-natured deity who embodied the harmony between land and sea, split apart during The Shattering to preserve his essence.

Temple Offerings: Food offerings

Temple Features: Sanctuaries that seamlessly blend forest and aquatic elements, with living trees and plants growing alongside pools and flowing water. The central altar stands at the boundary between a small indoor forest and a clear pool, symbolizing the unity that was lost.

Before The Shattering: Athuri was a colossal amphibious entity whose upper body manifested as a forest canopy of intertwined branches and leaves, while his lower body was a vast network of coral reefs and underwater root systems.
Froggians Emerged from the waters where Athuri's oceanic essence dispersed, carrying ancient knowledge and wisdom. They are repositories of pre-Shattering knowledge and seek to preserve what remains of their creator's consciousness.
Satyrs Born from the forest floor where Athuri's woodland essence took root, now slowly succumbing to corruption. Their Wild Hunt grows more violent with each passing year as void energies seep through the soil.

Crator

The Master Artificer

The god of crafting and stonework, survived The Shattering within his mountain fortress, protected by ancient rune magic.

Temple Offerings: Livestock offerings

Temple Features: Structures following strict geometric principles laid down by Crator himself, with every angle, proportion, and measurement precisely calculated. Displays of exceptional craftsmanship serve as both inspiration and teaching tools.

After The Shattering: Crator remains trapped within his mountain sanctuary, unable to walk freely in the world but still able to guide his followers through strict codexes of discipline and meticulous training.
Finestorians Blue-skinned master craftsmen transformed by exposure to Crator's divine essence. Their society is built around guilds and workshops, where masters pass down their knowledge to apprentices through years of rigorous training.
Pasunkas Bald monks practicing stone spiritualism, making themselves like stone both mentally and physically. Through rigorous discipline and devotion to Crator's teachings, they transformed themselves into living embodiments of stone.

Shmalor

The Fractured Queen

Once the goddess of transitions and metamorphosis, her essence was shattered into countless fragments during The Shattering.

Temple Offerings: Human sacrifices (prisoners)

Temple Features: Architecture that seems to shift and change when not directly observed, with mirrors creating disorienting visual effects. Fractured mosaics depict Shmalor's shattering and the transformation of her followers.

Before The Shattering: Shmalor was the goddess of transitions and metamorphosis, guiding beings through the natural cycles of birth, growth, decay, and rebirth. Each fragment now embodies a different aspect of her domainâ€”decay, transformation, rebirthâ€”but lacks the harmonious whole.
Wormlings Parasitic entities connected to both Shmalor and the God Emperor. Some Wormling colonies, particularly those that have infiltrated Bloodelf society, worship both the God Emperor and Shmalor, seeing them as complementary forces of transformation and decay.
Some Bloodelves Some Bloodelves are drawn to Shmalor's aspects of transformation, finding common ground between their blood consumption and her domain of metamorphosis. These dual worshippers often serve as intermediaries between the followers of Aminion and Shmalor.

Temple Offerings: Human sacrifices (prisoners)

Temple Features: Structures that deliberately defy conventional geometry, with angles that shift when not observed and shadows that move independently of light sources. Void altars serve as focal points for the God Emperor's influence.

Nature: The God Emperor is not a deity in the traditional sense, but a cosmic entity that exists beyond conventional understanding. It devoured the fruit of creation, leaving fragments of its incomprehensible form behind in our reality.
Wormlings Direct fragments of the God Emperor's essence, parasitic entities working to prepare for his return. Each Wormling colony carries a sliver of the God Emperor's consciousness, a faint echo of its cosmic awareness that manifests as disturbing visions and an instinctive drive to reunite with their progenitor.

The Northmen

The Unaligned

Pragmatic humans with no direct divine connection, adapting to the post-Shattering world through flexibility and opportunism.
